Which path fits you?

Both are self-petitioned immigrant visas filed on Form I-140. They differ in who they are for, how you qualify, and how fast the green card line moves.

First preference

EB-1A — Extraordinary Ability

  • WhoTop of your field, with sustained acclaim
  • TestAny 3 of 10 criteria (or one major award)
  • DegreeNot required
  • Premium15 calendar days
  • Bulletin (Jun 2026)Current except China Apr 2023, India Dec 2022
  • Best whenYou have awards, press, judging, major contributions
Second preference

EB-2 NIW — National Interest Waiver

  • WhoAdvanced-degree pros with a nationally important endeavor
  • TestAdvanced degree or exceptional ability + all 3 Dhanasar prongs
  • DegreeAdvanced degree, or 3-of-6 exceptional ability
  • Premium45 calendar days
  • Bulletin (Jun 2026)Current except China Sep 2021, India Sep 2013
  • Best whenStrong credentials + clear U.S. impact, not "the very top"
What's identical: both self-petition on Form I-140 ($715 + $300 asylum fee = $1,015); premium processing is $2,965 either way; the green-card-stage fees are the same; and you may file the green card concurrently when your priority date is current. Rule of thumb: if your record screams "best in the field," aim EB-1A. If you have strong credentials and work of clear national importance, EB-2 NIW is the broader door. You can also file both — each is judged separately.

EB-2 NIW: The Three Prongs Gateway

Unlike EB-1A's "any 3 of 10," the NIW (Matter of Dhanasar) is an AND test — pass all three gates. First pick how you qualify for EB-2, then open each gate.

Path A

Advanced degree

Master's or higher, OR Bachelor's/HND + 5+ years progressive experience.

Path B

Exceptional ability

Meet at least 3 of 6 USCIS criteria. No graduate degree required.

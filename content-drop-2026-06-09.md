## Reddit

**Target subreddits:** r/immigration, r/h1b (also reasonable: r/f1visa)

**Title:** EB-1A vs EB-2 NIW: a plain-English breakdown for F-1/OPT folks who keep seeing these thrown around

**Body:**

A lot of people on F-1 or OPT hear "just do NIW" or "go for EB-1A" without anyone explaining what actually separates them. Here is the version I wish someone had given me earlier. Not legal advice, just how the two work.

Both are employment-based green card categories, and the big deal is that both can be self-petitioned. That means no employer sponsor and no PERM labor certification. You file for yourself. For people whose work situation is uncertain, that independence is the whole point.

**EB-2 NIW (National Interest Waiver):** You still need an advanced degree or exceptional ability, but you ask USCIS to waive the job-offer and labor-cert requirements because your work is in the national interest. It is judged on a three-part test from a case called Matter of Dhanasar: (1) your endeavor has substantial merit and national importance, (2) you are well positioned to advance it, and (3) on balance it benefits the US to waive the normal requirements. It rewards a clear, important body of work with evidence behind it.

**EB-1A (Extraordinary Ability):** Higher bar. You show sustained national or international acclaim, usually by meeting at least 3 of 10 regulatory criteria (awards, published material about you, judging others' work, original contributions of major significance, authorship, etc.), then surviving a "final merits" review. The leading case here is Kazarian, which split the analysis into "do you meet the criteria" and "taken together, is this actually extraordinary." Plenty of people meet 3 criteria on paper and still get denied at the final merits stage.

**How to think about choosing:** NIW tends to fit researchers, founders, and specialists doing important work who are not yet "top of the field" famous. EB-1A fits people who can document real acclaim and has the bonus of often being current when EB-2 retrogresses (relevant right now, since the June 2026 bulletin pushed EB-1 and EB-2 India back again). Many people build toward NIW first and grow into EB-1A.

Two things worth knowing in 2026: USCIS recently reaffirmed that the final green card step (adjustment of status) is discretionary, so strong, well-documented evidence matters more than ever. And evidence takes time to build, so the earlier you understand which criteria you are even close to, the better.

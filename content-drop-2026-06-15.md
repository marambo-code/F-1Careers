## 4. Reddit

**Suggested subreddits:** r/immigration, r/h1b (also viable: r/EB2_NIW if active)

**Title:** "If H-1B feels like your only option, here is the part most F-1 students miss about EB-1A and NIW"

**Body:**

A lot of people here treat the H-1B lottery as the only door, and then spend years anxious about a process they cannot influence. Worth knowing there are two employment-based green card paths that do not run on a lottery, and one of them does not even need an employer to sponsor you.

EB-2 NIW (National Interest Waiver): You waive the job-offer and labor-certification requirement by showing your work is in the national interest. The framework comes from a 2016 case called Matter of Dhanasar, which uses three prongs: (1) your endeavor has substantial merit and national importance, (2) you are well positioned to advance it, and (3) on balance it benefits the US to waive the normal requirements. You can self-petition. No employer sponsor required.

EB-1A (Extraordinary Ability): Higher bar, but also self-petition and no lottery. You meet at least 3 of 10 regulatory criteria (awards, publications, judging others' work, original contributions, etc.), and then USCIS does a "final merits" review. That two-step structure comes from a case called Kazarian. The standard of proof across these is "preponderance of the evidence" (more likely than not), from Matter of Chawathe, which is a lower bar than most people assume.

Two practical points that genuinely help:

1. Most students underestimate their own profile because they have never seen the actual rubric. Things like reviewing papers for a conference, a first-author publication, or leading a critical project can map to specific criteria you did not know counted.

2. These run in parallel to whatever else you are doing. You do not have to abandon H-1B to start building an NIW case.

Recent context worth being aware of: USCIS issued a memo on May 21, 2026 reaffirming that adjustment of status is discretionary, so a clean, well-documented case matters more than ever. And there is a proposed DHS rule in final review that could replace open-ended "duration of status" with a fixed admission period for F-1s. Not finalized yet, so do not panic, but it is a reason to stop waiting.

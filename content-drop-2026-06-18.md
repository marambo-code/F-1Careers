## 4. Reddit

**Suggested subreddits:** r/immigration, r/EB2_NIW (also r/h1b for crossover)

**Title:** "Eligible" and "approvable" are not the same thing for EB-1A and NIW. Here is what actually decides it.

**Body:**

I see a lot of posts that boil down to "I meet X criteria, am I good?" Worth clearing up a common misunderstanding, because it trips people up at the worst possible time.

Meeting the requirements is necessary but not sufficient. Two reasons:

1. Adjustment of status is discretionary. USCIS reaffirmed this again in a May 2026 policy memo. It is not new law, but it is a reminder that officers can deny a case that technically qualifies if the overall record is weak or inconsistent.

2. The standards themselves are about quality, not just counting boxes.
   - EB-1A: you need to satisfy at least 3 of 10 regulatory criteria, but there is also a final merits determination (this is the Kazarian two-step). You can "meet 3 criteria" with thin evidence and still fail the merits review.
   - NIW: it is the three Dhanasar prongs (substantial merit and national importance, well positioned to advance the endeavor, and that a waiver benefits the US on balance). Prong 2 in particular is an evidence game, not a checklist.

Practical takeaway: stop asking "do I qualify" and start asking "can I prove it cleanly." Map yourself against every criterion, find your strongest pathway, then spend the next year closing the two or three weakest spots (a missing publication record, weak evidence of original contribution, no clear leadership documentation, etc.). Cases are built over months, not the week before filing.

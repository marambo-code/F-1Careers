## 4. Reddit

**Target subreddits:** r/immigration, r/h1b (also consider r/USCIS)

**Title:** EB-1A vs EB-2 NIW for someone on OPT: how do you actually know which one fits?

**Body:**

I see this question constantly, so here is a plain-English breakdown that should help whether or not you ever pay anyone a cent.

Both EB-1A and EB-2 NIW are self-petitions. That is the key thing most people miss. You do not need an employer to sponsor you and you are not in the H-1B lottery for these. You are building a case about yourself.

**EB-1A (Extraordinary Ability)** is judged in two steps under a case called Kazarian. First, do you meet at least 3 of the 10 regulatory criteria (things like awards, published material about you, judging others' work, original contributions of major significance, scholarly articles, leading role in distinguished organizations, high salary, etc.)? Second, a "final merits" review of whether the total record actually shows sustained acclaim. People fail not because they lack 3 boxes, but because the evidence behind each box is thin.

**EB-2 NIW (National Interest Waiver)** uses a 3-prong test from a case called Dhanasar. (1) Your proposed endeavor has substantial merit and national importance. (2) You are well positioned to advance it. (3) On balance, it benefits the US to waive the normal job-offer/labor-cert requirement. NIW is often more reachable for strong researchers, founders, and specialized professionals who are not yet "top of the field" famous.

The evidentiary standard for both is "preponderance of the evidence" (from a case called Chawathe), which means more likely than not, not "beyond doubt." That matters because people over-build for a standard that is higher than what the law requires.

How to actually choose: if you have clear external recognition (awards, press, citations, judging, etc.), EB-1A is worth scoping. If your strength is the importance and impact of your work rather than fame, NIW usually fits better. Many people qualify for NIW first and grow into EB-1A.

Two honest caveats. A recent USCIS memo (May 2026) reaffirmed that adjustment of status is discretionary, so a strong, clean record matters more than ever. And always run your specific facts past a licensed immigration attorney before filing, because this is strategy, not legal advice.
